“People of Mureș in the Trenches of the Great War”

The exhibition “People of Mureș in the Trenches of the Great War” was nominated for the title “Exhibition of the Year 2022” by the Pulszky Society – Hungarian Museum Association. The project presents a complex journey through the history of the First World War, highlighting the involvement of the people and region of Mureș in the major events that began in 1914 and continued until 1922.

The theme of the exhibition is structured in stages and modules, designed to mark not only the centenary of the war, but also the approaching centenary of the coronation of the Romanian sovereigns. The project uses a multidisciplinary approach, combining perspectives from history, archaeology, ethnology and anthropology, through modern museological methods.

Visitors are invited to rediscover fragments of everyday life during wartime: emotions, perceptions, fears and hopes experienced both on the front lines and behind the front, as well as in the period immediately following the war.

The exhibition experience is enhanced through interactive museum techniques, digital elements and info-touch screens that include archival documents, historical photographs and rare materials. This creates a powerful emotional impact thanks to the professional visual presentation and the use of digital technology.

Historical perspective of the exhibition

The idea of the exhibition dates back to 2017 and connects the First World War with the Centenary of the Great Union. The exhibition was created through the cooperation of three museum sections (history, ethnography and archaeology), a rare collaboration in the museum field.

Four thematic units make up the exhibition: soldiers’ impressions from the front, the effects of war on the civilian population, the political changes in Târgu Mureș and Mureș County, and the connection between war trenches and aerial archaeology.

The section presenting the war does not strictly follow chronology, but focuses on the experiences of the ordinary soldier, regardless of army or nationality. To enhance authenticity, one unit recreates the atmosphere of a trench, while the impressions are conveyed through visual and sound effects.

The final hall presents the political events between 1918 and 1922, including the collapse of autumn 1918, revolutionary movements and the political changes that followed. The climax of the events was the coronation of King Ferdinand in 1922 as the ruler of Greater Romania.

Perspective of aerial archaeology

Viewed from above, the landscapes at the foot of the Eastern Carpathians still preserve traces of the confrontations of the Great War. By studying the landscape and interpreting the terrain, defensive systems and trench lines can be identified.

In the present-day Mureș County area, a secondary defensive line of the Transylvanian trench system extended from Brâncovenești toward Adrian – Mătrici – Ghindari, connecting to the main defensive alignment in the valley of the Târnava Mică.

Through research and mapping based on sources and field observations, the exhibition offers a deeper understanding of how the traces of the war remained embedded in the local landscape and contributed to the historical knowledge of the region.
