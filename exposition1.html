Traditional Fairs

The exhibition was created in 2013 within the cultural project Traditional Fairs – places of commercial and cultural exchanges, funded by the National Cultural Fund Administration. The exhibition presents the important role that traditional fairs played in the economic and social life of the communities in Mureș County.

The main idea of the exhibition is commercial exchange but also cultural dialogue. The fair was the place where people met to sell and buy products, but also to exchange information, customs and traditions.

The connection between the rural and urban worlds is suggested through the variety of goods sold at fairs: animals, cereals, agricultural products, handcrafted objects and household goods.

The variety of traditional costumes displayed in the exhibition illustrates the multicultural character of the Mureș area and the influences between the different communities that have lived here over time.

The exhibition is organized in three thematic halls and presents the evolution of annual fairs and rural fairs from the Mureș region, from the interwar period until today.
