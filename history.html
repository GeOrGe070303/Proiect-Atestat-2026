Our History

The Mureș County Museum was established to preserve and promote the cultural and historical heritage of Mureș County. Over time, the museum has brought together significant collections of ethnography, archaeology, history, and art, reflecting the evolution of local communities. The museum building and its exhibitions have been continuously developed, adapting to modern requirements for the conservation and presentation of heritage. Today, the museum represents an important cultural center, contributing to public education and the preservation of the region’s cultural identity.

The building’s patron was Count László Toldalagi, advisor to the Royal Table. Construction began in 1759 and took place in several stages until 1772, when it was fully completed, including the rear wing. This wing was later transformed into the current Ethnography Museum building by Aurel Filimon in the spring of 1921.
